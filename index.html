Note → color map

Adjacent slices are a fifth apart (C→G→D…). Harmonious notes sit near each other, so chords blend into pleasing colors.

Controls

Each note keeps its own color, blended across a gradient.
Rotates each octave's hue a few degrees, so the same note reads as a distinct tint per octave.
Paints each piano key with its mapped note color.
Play with your mouse, or use your computer keyboard: A W S E D F T G Y H U J K for one octave.

Listen & Paint

Turn on your microphone and the screen paints itself from what it hears. Pitch becomes hue, loudness becomes brightness, and tone shapes the texture.
